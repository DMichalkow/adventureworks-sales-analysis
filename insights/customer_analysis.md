# Customer Insights

## Customer Revenue Distribution

The majority of customers belong to the **Low** and **Medium** revenue segments.  
The **Low segment contains over 10,000 customers**, while the **Medium segment includes nearly 7,800 customers**.

Despite their large numbers, these customers generate relatively small individual revenue compared to high-value customers.

This indicates that the company has a **large base of low-spending customers**.

---

## High-Value Customers

A relatively small group of **VIP customers (323 customers)** generates a disproportionately large share of total revenue.

Total revenue generated by VIP customers exceeds **86 million**, which is significantly higher than revenue generated by other segments.

This suggests that **a small portion of customers contributes heavily to overall sales**, reflecting a typical Pareto-like distribution.

---

## Top Customers

The top-performing customers generate extremely high revenue individually.

For example:
- The top customer generated **~989,000 in total revenue**
- The top 10 customers each generated between **~820,000 and ~990,000**

These customers also tend to place **multiple orders (often around 12 orders)**, indicating strong and consistent purchasing activity.

---

## Customer Segmentation

Customer segmentation based on total revenue shows the following structure:

| Segment | Customers | Revenue |
|-------|-------|-------|
| VIP | 323 | ~86.8M |
| High Value | 182 | ~3.8M |
| Medium | 7,796 | ~30.9M |
| Low | 10,818 | ~1.68M |

Key observations:

- **VIP customers generate the majority of total revenue**
- **Medium customers represent a large portion of the customer base**
- **Low-value customers are numerous but contribute relatively little revenue**

---

## Business Implications

The results suggest that the company should focus on:

- **retaining VIP customers**, as they generate a significant share of revenue
- **developing Medium customers**, who represent a large potential for revenue growth
- analyzing whether **Low-value customers can be converted into higher-value segments**
