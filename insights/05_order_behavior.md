# Order Behaviour Insights

## Average Order Size

The analysis shows that the **average order contains approximately 3 items**.

This indicates that customers typically purchase **small product bundles rather than single-item orders**.  
The basket size suggests moderate cross-selling activity, where customers add multiple items to a single order.

---

## Average Order Value (AOV)

The **average order value is approximately 3,916**.

Additional observations:

- Minimum order value: **~1.52**
- Maximum order value: **~187,487**

The extremely high maximum order value indicates that some transactions are **significantly larger than typical customer purchases**, likely representing bulk or high-value orders.

This suggests the presence of **outlier orders or large business purchases**.

---

## Order Growth Over Time

The number of orders increases significantly over the analyzed period.

Key observations:

- Early years (2011–2012) show relatively **low order volumes**.
- Starting in **2013**, the number of orders grows rapidly.
- The highest activity occurs in **2014**, where monthly orders exceed **2,000**.

This indicates **strong business growth and increasing demand over time**.

---

## Order Seasonality

Monthly order distribution shows moderate seasonality.

Key patterns:

- The highest number of orders occurs in **May (~3175 orders)** and **March (~3144 orders)**.
- The lowest activity occurs in **June (~2189 orders)**.

Overall, order volumes remain relatively stable across most months, suggesting **consistent demand throughout the year with slight peaks in spring months**.

---

## Operational Implications

The analysis suggests several operational insights:

- Customers typically purchase **small baskets (~3 items per order)**.
- The business experienced **significant order growth between 2012 and 2014**.
- Spring months appear to generate **slightly higher order volumes**, indicating potential seasonal demand.
- Large outlier orders suggest that **some high-value customers or bulk purchases contribute significantly to revenue**.
